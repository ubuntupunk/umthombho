    # Welcome to DeCap! 🎉


    Hey there, and welcome to the Netbones DeCap community! We're absolutely thrilled to have you here. Whether you're a seasoned developer or just getting started with content management systems, you've made a fantastic choice, and we can't wait to see what you'll build.


    ## Netbones South Africa ❤️ Open Source


    At **Netbones South Africa**, we're incredibly proud to associate ourselves with DeCap and the open-source movement. After careful evaluation, we've chosen DeCap as our preferred content manager for our SPA (Single Page Application) rollout. Why? Because we believe in the power of open-source technology to deliver robust, flexible, and community-driven solutions that put you in control.


    ## Getting Started: Your First Steps


    DeCap is a powerful, open-source content management system that gives you complete control over your content without the hassle of a traditional database. It's Git-based, which means your content lives right alongside your code — versioned, trackable, and totally portable.


    Here's the best part: you get a sleek, user-friendly admin interface for your static site without sacrificing the speed and security that static sites are known for. Pretty cool, right?


    ## Meet Your New Best Friend: The Content Manager


    The **Content Manager** is where the magic happens! This is your command center for creating, editing, and organizing all your content. Think of it as your creative workspace — clean, intuitive, and built to get out of your way so you can focus on what matters: your content.


    Here's what you can do:


    * **Create and edit content** with a beautiful, distraction-free editor

    * **Preview your changes** before publishing (because nobody likes surprises)

    * **Manage media files** with drag-and-drop simplicity

    * **Organize content** using collections that match your site's structure

    * **Collaborate with your team** using editorial workflows (if you've set them up)


    To access your Content Manager, simply navigate to `/admin` on your site. You'll be greeted by a login screen, and once authenticated, you're ready to roll!


    ## Your Step-by-Step Guide to Creating Content


    Let's walk through creating your first piece of content together. Don't worry - it's easier than it looks!


    ### Understanding Your Workspace: Edit vs Preview


    When you create a new piece of content, you'll notice two main areas at the top of your screen:


    **📝 Edit Mode** - This is where you write and format your content. Think of it as your writing desk - it shows all the fields you need to fill in (title, body text, images, etc.). This is where you'll spend most of your time.


    **👁️ Preview Mode** - Click this to see exactly how your content will appear on the live website. It's like looking at a printed proof before sending something to press. No fields, no forms - just the final result.


    **Pro Tip:** Toggle between Edit and Preview frequently as you work. It helps catch formatting issues early and gives you confidence that everything looks great!


    ### Working with Languages: Your Multilingual Toolkit


    You'll see a **language dropdown** at the top of your content editor. We have added Xhosa as one of the international locales. This is one of DeCap's most powerful features, and here's how to use it:


    #### Creating Content in Multiple Languages


    1. **Select your primary language** from the dropdown (e.g., "English")

    2. **Fill in all your content** - title, body, images, everything

    3. **Save your work** (very important!)

    4. **Switch to another language** using the dropdown (e.g., "Xhosa")

    5. **Translate your content** - the same fields will appear, but empty and ready for your translation

    6. **Save again** - each language version is saved separately


    #### Understanding Language Locales


    When you see the language dropdown, you'll notice codes like:


    * **English (en)** - This is your English locale. Use standard English spelling and grammar.

    * **Xhosa (xh)** - This is your Xhosa locale. Input your content in isiXhosa here.


    **Important:** Each language version is completely independent. Changing the English version won't automatically update the Xhosa version - you'll need to update each language separately. This gives you full control over how your message is conveyed in each language!


    ### Your Duel Medium Content Creation Checklist


    Every time you create a new piece of content, follow these steps:


    ✅ **Step 1:** Click "New \[Content Type]" from your collection


    ✅ **Step 2:** Select your starting language (usually English)


    ✅ **Step 3:** Fill in all required fields (marked with a red asterisk *)


    ✅ **Step 4:** Click Preview to check how it looks


    ✅ **Step 5:** Save your work


    ✅ **Step 6:** Switch to Xhosa using the language dropdown


    ✅ **Step 7:** Translate all the content into isiXhosa


    ✅ **Step 8:** Preview the Xhosa version


    ✅ **Step 9:** Save again


    ✅ **Step 10:** Publish when both versions are ready!


    ### Need Help?

    ## Pro Tips to Get You Started


    1. **Save your work often** - The Content Manager autosaves, but it's always good to hit that save button regularly, especially on longer posts.

    2. **Use the preview feature** - It's your safety net! Always check how your content will look before hitting publish.

    3. **Organize with categories and tags** - Keep your content structured and easy to find later.

    4. **Master the media library** - Take time to organize your images into folders. Your future self will thank you!

    5. **Join the community** - The DeCap community is friendly, helpful, and always eager to welcome newcomers. Don't hesitate to ask questions!
